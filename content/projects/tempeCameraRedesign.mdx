## Overview

The Tempe Camera Website redesign was a part of completely re-envisioning the website of Tempe camera here in Phoenix. Their current website is really bad with no thought behind the design. We made it functional, interactive, allowing the users to see the products and its details properly, with plans of making the backend cataloging automated if the redesign goes further to building a microservice for them. It also had a better flow since by allowing people to make accounts it could cater a more personalized experience and since they offer student discounts to kids from ASU it is much simpler for them to just make an account and then verify it from there to reduce friction when renting or buying from Tempe Camera.

## Role

**UX Designer - solo project.** Responsible for research, wireframing, visual design, and prototyping.

## Tools

- Figma - wireframes, high-fidelity mockups, interactive prototype

## Problem

The existing Tempe Camera website presented friction across multiple stages of the user journey:

- **Product discovery** was unclear - category navigation lacked structure and filtering was absent
- **Product pages** didn't effectively surface camera specifications or enable comparison between models
- **Checkout** had unnecessary steps and limited payment flexibility, increasing drop-off risk

The goal was to redesign the experience holistically rather than patch individual issues.

## Process

1. Audited the existing site to identify friction points across discovery, product pages, and checkout
2. Developed low-fidelity wireframes to establish new information architecture and user flows
3. Iterated to high-fidelity mockups covering the full site: homepage, category pages, product detail pages, cart, and checkout
4. Built a fully interactive prototype in Figma simulating the complete purchase journey

## Key Design Decisions

**Product Discovery** - Redesigned category navigation with a filter system allowing users to browse by sensor type, price range, and brand. Eliminated ambiguity about where to start.

**Product Page** - Overhauled the layout to lead with hero imagery, surfaced key specs above the fold, and added a side-by-side comparison feature so users can evaluate cameras without leaving the page.

**Checkout** - Simplified to three steps: cart review → payment → confirmation. Removed redundant form fields and added support for multiple payment methods including digital wallets.

**Visual Hierarchy** - Standardised typography and spacing across all pages for a more coherent and professional brand experience.

## Outcome

A complete high-fidelity Figma prototype demonstrating a redesigned end-to-end purchase experience for Tempe Camera - covering homepage, category browsing, product detail pages, cart management, and checkout confirmation.
